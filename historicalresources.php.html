A FACELESS MAN

The faith and fate of Girolamo Donzellini.

A non-profit documentary and public history project by Dr. Alessandra Celati and Dr. Emma Hinchliffe

Historical resources and bibliography

The history of Girolamo Donzellini can be reconstructed by searching the Venetian Inquisition archive. Here you can find a list of the sources related to Donzellini’s life, network and medical-philosophical outputs. Main original historical records
Archivio di stato di Venezia, Sant’Uffizio, Processi, Contro Girolamo Donzellini, Busta 39

Archivio di stato di Venezia, Sant’Uffizio, Processi, Contro Nascimbene Nascimbeni, Busta 30

Archivio di stato di Venezia, Sant’Uffizio, Processi, Contro Agostino Gadaldino, Busta 13

Archivio di stato di Venezia, Sant’Uffizio, Processi, Contro Lucio Paolo Rosello, Busta 10

Archivio di stato di Venezia, Sant’Uffizio, Processi, Contro fra’ Teodoro da Capodistria, Busta 3

Archivio di stato di Venezia, Sant’Uffizio, Processi, Fascicolo no. 3, 1553, Busta 11

Basel, Universitätsbibliothek, Abteilung Frey-Gryn Mscr I and II (letters to Theodor Zwinger)

Erlangen, Universitätsbibliothek, Briefsammlung Trew (letters to Joaquim Camerarius)

Donzellini’s medical and philosophical books quoted in the documentary
Girolamo Donzellini, Themisti Euphradae philosophi peripatetici orationes octo elegantissimae, ac eruditione varia refertissimae (Basel: Perna, 1559).

Girolamo Donzellini, Discorso nobilissimo e dottissimo, preservativo e curativo della peste (Venice, Orazio Gobi di Salò: 1577).

Girolamo Donzellini, Remendium ferendarum iniuriarum (Venice: Ziletti, 1586).

Very essential bibliography on the history of religious dissent in sixteenth century Venice and Italy, and on the other main themes addressed in the documentary
Delio Cantimori, Eretici italiani del Cinquecento e Prospettive di storia ereticale, Adriano Prosperi (ed.), (Turin: Einaudi, 2009, first edition 1939)

Paul F. Grendler, The Roman Inquisition and the Venetian Press, 1540-1605 (Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1975)

Massimo Firpo, Riforma protestante ed eresie nell’Italia del ‘500: un profilo storico (Rome – Bari: Laterza, 1993)

John Martin, Venice’s Hidden Enemies. Italian Heretics in A Renaissance City, (Berkeley-Los Angeles: University of California Press, 1993)

Carlos Gilly, Theodor Zwinger e la crisi culturale della seconda metà del Cinquecento, provisional draft, 2012, available here

Alessandra Celati, The World of Girolamo Donzellini. A Network of Heterodox Physicians in Sixteenth-Century Venice (London: Routledge, at the time when this website was under construction the book was forthcoming – due in 2022)
