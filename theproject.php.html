A FACELESS MAN

The faith and fate of Girolamo Donzellini.

A non-profit documentary and public history project by Dr. Alessandra Celati and Dr. Emma Hinchliffe

About us and the project

This non-profit documentary is the result of a chance encounter between two young historians of early-modern history, Alessandra Celati and Emma Hinchliffe, who met at an academic conference at the Huntington Library in 2018. Believing that the past has significant value in the present, Alessandra and Emma share the same strong passion for telling stories from the past, especially for a public audience. They want these stories to be known outside an academic context, so that the significance of history can reach beyond the academy to improve public understanding of the past and the world that we have inherited. Celati and Hinchliffe believe that the story conveyed in this documentary is an important and vital one that deserves to be told. Donzelinis' perseverance and diligence to speak truth to power shines through regardless of his tragic end and provides a prescient lesson for the modern world about the dangers of dogma and authoritarianism and the value of critical thinking and freedom of expression.
